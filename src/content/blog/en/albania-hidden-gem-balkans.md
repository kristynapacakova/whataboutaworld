Albania, a small country located on the Balkan Peninsula, is often overlooked by travelers seeking adventures and beauty in southeastern Europe. However, this country, known for its rich history, stunning nature, and hospitable people, offers visitors many reasons to explore it. If you have never been to Albania and are looking for useful information, here are some key points you should know.

## Culture and History 🌄

Albania has a rich and diverse history, ranging from ancient Illyrians, through the periods of the Roman and Ottoman Empires, to modern times. The country is home to many historical landmarks, including ancient cities like Butrint and Gjirokastër, which are UNESCO World Heritage sites. Albanians are proud of their cultural identity and traditions, which you can experience everywhere - from traditional music and dance, to local cuisine and the hospitality they offer.

## Nature and Beaches 🌊

One of Albania's biggest attractions is its natural beauty. The country offers stunning mountains, lakes, and rivers, ideal for hiking, cycling, and other outdoor activities. The Albanian coastline along the Adriatic and Ionian seas is dotted with beautiful beaches that remain relatively undiscovered and are not crowded with tourists. Beaches like Ksamil, Dhermi, and Himara are known for their clear waters and picturesque scenery.

## Practical Information 💡

- **Visas and entry:** EU citizens do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days.
- **Currency:** Albanian Lek (ALL). In tourist areas, euros are often accepted.
- **Language:** The official language is Albanian, but in tourist areas, you can often communicate in English or Italian.
- **Transport:** Albania has a growing network of buses and shared taxis. Renting a car is also a common and convenient option for exploring the country.

## Driving in Albania 🚗

If you plan to drive in Albania, it is recommended to have an International Driving Permit (IDP) in addition to your national driving license. The IDP is recognized in Albania and can be useful in case of any encounters with local authorities or car rental agencies.

## Tips for Travelers ✈️

- **Explore off-season:** The main tourist season is in the summer, when the beaches are most visited. For a quieter vacation, consider visiting in spring or fall.
- **Try local cuisine:** Albanian cuisine is influenced by Mediterranean and Balkan traditions. Try traditional dishes like byrek, tave kosi, or fresh seafood.
- **Respect local customs:** Albanians are known for their hospitality and respect for traditions. Being polite and respecting local customs will ensure you have a pleasant experience.

Albania is a country that offers something for every traveler. Whether you're seeking adventure in nature, relaxation on the beach, or cultural enrichment, Albania will not disappoint. Let yourself be enchanted by the magic of this hidden gem of the Balkans and discover all that it has to offer.
